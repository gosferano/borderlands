About

This is a page dedicated to providing information about the Borderlands games, including character builds, game data, and other related content. While build rating might be subjective, the goal is to offer well-researched and community-vetted builds to help players optimize their gameplay experience. Thus, user feedback and contributions are highly encouraged to ensure the content remains relevant and useful.

If you have any suggestions or feedback please open a GitHub issue for discussion.

Build testing and review guidelines

Star rating

Builds are rated on a scale of 0 to 4 stars in two categories: mobbing and bossing. Mobbing refers to the build's effectiveness against groups of enemies, while bossing evaluates its performance against single, powerful foes. The ratings are determined based on various factors, including damage output, survivability, utility, and overall fun factor. A higher star rating indicates a more effective and enjoyable build for the respective category.

Meaning of the star ratings is as follows:

  • - exceptionally good
  • - better than average
  • - worse than average
  • - exceptionally bad
  • - unusable

Tagging

Builds can be tagged with relevant keywords to help categorize and identify their primary focus or playstyle. Tags can include character names, specific strategies, or gameplay phases such as "leveling" or "endgame." Proper tagging ensures that builds are easily discoverable by users searching for specific types of builds or strategies. All tags should be lower case.

Here are some rules to follow when tagging builds:

  • Always include character name in lowercase
  • Always include either `leveling` and/or `endgame`
  • Add action skill name
  • Add legendary class mode name, if used
  • Add notable skill name
  • Add notable weapon type or name
  • DLC name(s) if required (for either gear or character)
